What is Lowboy Trucking?

A heavy haul lowboy trailer is a semi-trailer with a low bed, which is known as a drop neck. A single heavy haul lowboy trailer has two drops, which helps it to sit close to the ground. One drop is in the front of the wheels, and the second is just behind the gooseneck. Its qualities make lowboy trucking ideal for heavy hauling services.

Types of Lowboy Trucking Trailers

There are various types of lowboy trucking trailers that are available at Auto Transportation Center. The gooseneck method and trailer-attached method are the most popular types of lowboy trucking.

Fixed neck lowboy

This trailer is furnished with a fixed-neck lowboy trailer. This is a lightweight alternative feature. Due to its fixed style, it is difficult to load from the front of the trailer.

Fixed gooseneck lowboy trailer

Fixed gooseneck lowboys are fit out with drop decks, which allow easy loading from the front and the back. Because the gooseneck is adjustable, and once you reattach it, it securely holds the cargo in the position.

RGN - Removable Gooseneck Lowboy

This is the most trusted and robust vehicle for heavy hauling services. Removable gooseneck trailers are, also known as RGN, which provide 20 axles by which it has the capability to transport the cargo with a maximum weight of 150000.

Advantages of using lowboy trucking services

There are various benefits of using a lowboy trucking trailer for the heavy hauling industry and transportation requirements.

Height clearance

Lowboy trucking trailers have a deck close to the ground, which helps in carrying the tall types of equipment. This is best when you are transporting wind turbine components, cranes, or other oversized types of equipment.

Security

Due to its lower center of gravity, it enhances stability and minimizes swaying and tipping. Lowboy trucking trailers are equipped with multiple axles and a specialized suspension system to give your cargo a safe and secure ride.

Safety

Lowboy trailers are designed to load and unload heavy equipment easily and safely. Ramps or hydraulic systems help greatly to assist this process. This reduces the risk of accidents and injuries while loading and unloading.

Cost effective
